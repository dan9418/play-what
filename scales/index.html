Scales

The foundation of melody

Scale Basics

A scale is a group of notes played sequentially. They are the basic building blocks of melody.

Types of Scales

  • Diatonic
  • Pentatonic
  • Blues
  • Bebop

Common Scales

  • Major (Ionian)
  • Natural Minor (Aeolian)
  • Melodic Minor
  • Harmonic Minor
  • Major Pentatonic
  • Minor Pentatonic

Diatonic Modes

Diatonic scales are the most common type in Western music.

Examples

  • Ionian
  • Dorian
  • Phrygian
  • Lydian
  • Mixolydian
  • Aeolian
  • Locrian

Harmonic Minor Modes

Harmonic minor scales are...

Examples

  • Harmonic Minor
  • Locrian 6
  • Ionian #5
  • Dorian #4
  • Phrygian Dominant
  • Lydian #2
  • Super Locrian bb7

Melodic Minor Modes

Melodic minor scales are...

Examples

  • Melodic Minor
  • Dorian b2
  • Lydian #5
  • Lydian Dominant
  • Mixolydian b6
  • Aeolian b5
  • Super Locrian (Altered)

Pentatonic

Pentatonic scales contains 5 notes.

Examples

  • Major Pentatonic
  • Egyptian
  • Man Gong
  • Ritusen
  • Minor Pentatonic

Blues

Blues scales are pentatonic scales with an extra note.

Examples

  • Major Blues
  • Minor Blues

Bebop

Bebop scales are diatonic scales with an extra note.

Examples

  • Major Bebop
  • Dominant Bebop
